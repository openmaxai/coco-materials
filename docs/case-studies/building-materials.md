## Background: <em>A Distribution Business Under Pressure</em>

This Singapore-based building materials distributor serves construction contractors and engineering firms across the island, with a product catalog spanning structural materials, finishing materials, and specialty items. Customer communication runs primarily through WhatsApp and WeCom, and during peak construction seasons the volume is relentless.

The company had reached an inflection point familiar to many mid-sized distributors: business was growing, but the back-office operations powering it had not scaled. Every new contractor relationship meant more messages to triage, more invoices to process, and more quantity estimates to produce. The team was spending its days on data entry and message monitoring rather than on the high-value work of building client relationships and closing deals.

What made the situation particularly acute was the competitive dynamics of Singapore's construction supply market. In building materials, speed is the differentiator. Contractors send an inquiry to three or four distributors simultaneously, and the first one to respond with an accurate quote almost always wins the order. Falling behind on response time did not just mean inefficiency — it meant lost revenue.

</div>

<div class="case-section">

## Challenges: <em>Three Bottlenecks Compounding Daily</em>

The distributor faced three interlocking operational bottlenecks that reinforced each other, creating a cycle of missed opportunities and growing costs.

First, the **message backlog** had become unmanageable. With 200+ WhatsApp messages arriving every day — price inquiries, order confirmations, delivery follow-ups, after-sales requests — the team was perpetually behind. Manual review meant messages sat unread for hours, and every missed inquiry represented a direct revenue loss to a faster competitor.

Second, **invoice processing** had hit a hard ceiling. Manual data entry limited throughput to roughly 30 invoices per day, regardless of demand. During peak season, the backlog would grow for days, delaying client billing cycles and straining cash flow. Staff overtime could not meaningfully move the needle because the bottleneck was the manual process itself.

Third, the **quantity surveying workflow** was both expensive and slow. Every QS estimate required engaging an external Quantity Surveyor at SGD 3,000 to 5,000 per engagement, with turnaround times of 5 to 7 working days. For a business that needed to quote quickly to win orders, waiting a week for a cost estimate was competitively untenable.

</div>

<div class="case-section">

## Solution: <em>An Integrated AI Capability Layer</em>

OpenMax built an integrated AI capability layer covering three core operational areas: front-end customer communication, invoice processing, and construction quantity estimation. Rather than deploying three separate point solutions, OpenMax delivered a unified AI system that shares context across all three workflows.
