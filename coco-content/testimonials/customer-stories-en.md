## Case 5: CoCo's Own CRM — Built by AI, Run by AI

**What was built:** A 7-sheet Lark CRM tracking the full customer lifecycle for CoCo's own sales team
**Who built it:** Zylos, an AI agent running on the CoCo platform
**Users tracked:** 27 trial and paid customers

This one is worth telling because it's CoCo eating its own cooking.

CoCo's team needed a way to track 27 users moving through intake → trial → follow-up → conversion. Rather than buying a SaaS tool or spending weeks on a spreadsheet, the team had their AI agent, Zylos, design and build the entire system.

The result: a 7-sheet Lark Sheets CRM with three automated daily pipelines running on top of it.

**What runs automatically every day:**

**10:00 AM — Daily Expiry Check.** The system scans every user's trial status, categorizes them by urgency (expiring today, expiring this week, already lapsed), generates a prioritized follow-up list, and posts it directly to the team's Lark group. No one opens a spreadsheet. The briefing comes to them.

**Every 30 minutes — Payment Monitoring.** Stripe sends a notification whenever a payment processes. Zylos picks up those notifications, reconciles them against the CRM, updates the paid user sheet (handling multi-currency: USD and SGD), and logs the change. Zero manual entry. The moment someone pays, the CRM reflects it.

**08:30 AM on the 1st — Monthly Calendar Refresh.** Once a month, the system rebuilds a color-coded status board for the new period — categorizing every user's current state so the team starts each month with a clean, current picture.

**The outcome:**

- 9 out of 27 users converted to paid — a **33% trial-to-paid conversion rate** (the SaaS industry average is roughly 10%)
- Every payment update, every status change, every follow-up list: AI-generated, AI-posted, zero manual data entry
- The entire pipeline from Stripe notification → CRM update → team notification → follow-up prioritization runs without a human touching it

> *[Illustrative quote — reflects team sentiment]* "We didn't build this CRM. We described what we needed, and it existed the next day. The weird part is it actually works better than the CRM we were considering buying."
